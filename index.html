Overview - KIMS

Kempegowda Institute of Medical Sciences (KIMS) was established in 1980 and affiliated with Rajiv Gandhi University of Health Sciences (RGUHS), Karnataka. KIMS aims to provide an insightful and holistic educational experience that prepares students for careers in medicine through excellence in teaching, patient care, and research. It focuses on training students to acquire knowledge, skills, and attitude, to excel as physicians with abilities to be globally relevant and cater to the medical needs of society.

Reasons to study at KIMS!

KIMS provides high-quality education by providing top-notch academic infrastructure and high-tech facilities for the overall growth and development of the students.
The college is specializes in medical sciences such as MBBS, MD/MS, and Para-medical and offers a wide range of courses in medical education.
It has in-house expertise faculties who come with a wealth of knowledge and expertise. All of the in-house professors are practitioners at the hospital with doctorates or PhDs.

    Eligibility Criteria

    Under-Graduate

    MBBS
    • Candidates must be 17 years old by December 31st of the admission year.
    • Passed the PUC exam conducted by a recognized board with PCB as compulsory subject. OR
    • Passed the BSc examination of Bangalore University or any other statutory University with Physics, Chemistry, Botany, and Zoology as mandatory subjects.
    • Candidates must have secured a minimum of 50% total marks in Science subjects altogether at the qualifying examination and qualified any one entrance exam: CET/COMED-K/NEET UG.

    For foreign students,

    • A NOC from the Department of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India, New Delhi must be produced.
    • Student Visa
    • Residential Certificate

    Post-Graduate

    MD/MS
    • Students seeking admission must have earned an MBBS degree from a recognized Medical College affiliated with any university recognized by MCI.
    • The Candidates must have qualified PGCET/COMED-K/NEET PG exams.
    • Candidates must have completed a 1-year rotating internship in a teaching institution recognized by MCI.
    • The Candidates must have registered their MBBS degree with the respective State Medical Council.

    Para-Medical

    • The Minimum Age is 15 years and the Upper Age limit is 35 Years. If the Candidate belongs to SC/ST, a relaxation of up to 40 Years is given.

      • For All Para-Medical Courses except Dip.Medical Record Technology

    • Passed in II PUC or equivalent examination with Science subject ( Physics, Chemistry and Biology /Botany /Zoology ) OR Passed in SSLC / X – Standard / Equivalent Examination.

      • For Medical Record Technology,

    • Passed in II PUC/Equivalent examination with any subjects (PCMC/PCME/Commerce/Arts) OR Passed in SSLC/Equivalent examination.
